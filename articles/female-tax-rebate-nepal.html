The 10% Female Tax Rebate in Nepal: Who Qualifies and How It Works

Calculator All Articles

Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

Nepal's tax code includes a rebate aimed at women whose income comes solely from employment — a detail that's easy to miss when estimating tax by hand, and one this calculator applies automatically when selected.

Who qualifies

The rebate applies to a woman taxpayer whose income for the year consists entirely of remuneration — meaning salary and employment income, with no other income sources like business profit, rental income, or capital gains mixed in. If income includes anything beyond employment earnings, the rebate typically does not apply for that year.

How the rebate is calculated

Unlike a deduction, which reduces taxable income before slab rates are applied, this rebate is applied after the tax has already been calculated. It reduces the final tax payable by 10%.

Worked example

A woman with NPR 800,000 in salary income (no other income sources) has a calculated tax of roughly NPR 35,000 under the standard single-filer slabs.
With the rebate applied: NPR 35,000 × 0.9 = NPR 31,500 payable — a saving of NPR 3,500.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming it applies automatically. Employers don't always apply this rebate correctly during monthly TDS deduction — it's worth checking your payslip or year-end tax statement.
  • Mixing income types. Freelance income, rental income, or business income alongside a salary can disqualify the year from the rebate, even if the salary portion would have qualified alone.
  • Confusing it with other deductions. This rebate stacks with deductions like SSF/EPF contributions and insurance premiums — it's calculated on top of, not instead of, those.

If you're unsure whether your specific income mix qualifies, it's worth confirming with a chartered accountant or your employer's payroll/HR team, since misapplying this rebate can mean either overpaying or under-withholding TDS through the year.
