This calculator exists because working out Nepali income tax by hand — matching the right slab, applying the SSF/insurance deduction caps, remembering the female rebate — is fiddly enough that most people either overpay carelessly or underestimate what they'll owe.

I built it as a small, independent side project, not on behalf of the Inland Revenue Department or any accounting firm. The goal is simple: give anyone in Nepal a fast, free way to estimate their annual tax and monthly TDS using the current Finance Act slabs, without needing an account, a login, or a spreadsheet.

How it's kept accurate

Nepal's tax slabs are set fresh each fiscal year through the Finance Act, usually announced around Jestha/Ashadh for the year starting mid-July. This site's rates are checked and updated each year against the official Finance Act and Inland Revenue Department publications. If you ever spot a figure that looks outdated, the contact page has a way to reach me.

What this site is not

It's not a substitute for a chartered accountant, and it doesn't file anything on your behalf. Edge cases — non-resident status, presumptive/business tax, capital gains, disability credits — aren't modelled here. Think of it as a fast first estimate, not a final answer.
