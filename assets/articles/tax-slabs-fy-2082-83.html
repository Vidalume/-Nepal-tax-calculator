Nepal Income Tax Slabs for FY 2082/83, Explained

Calculator All Articles

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read

Nepal taxes individual income progressively — different portions of your income are taxed at different rates, rather than one flat rate applying to everything you earn. Understanding how the slabs actually stack is the difference between a rough guess and an accurate estimate of what you owe.

The slabs for single filers

Couples get a wider 1% and 10% band than single filers, which is why choosing the correct filing status matters before you estimate anything.

Why "progressive" trips people up

A common misunderstanding is assuming that if your income lands in the 20% band, your entire income is taxed at 20%. That's not how it works. Only the portion of income that falls inside each band is taxed at that band's rate — everything below it is still taxed at the lower rates.

Worked example: single filer earning NPR 900,000

First NPR 500,000 at 1% = NPR 5,000
Next NPR 200,000 (500,000–700,000) at 10% = NPR 20,000
Next NPR 200,000 (700,000–900,000) at 20% = NPR 40,000
Total tax: NPR 65,000 — an effective rate of about 7.2%, even though the top slab touched is 20%.

The 1% Social Security Tax isn't always payable

If you already contribute to the Social Security Fund (SSF), or you're a pensioner, or a registered sole proprietor, the 1% on the first slab is typically waived rather than charged on top of your SSF contribution. This is one of the most commonly missed adjustments when people estimate their tax by hand.

Deductions reduce taxable income before slabs apply

Retirement contributions, life insurance premiums, health insurance premiums, and remote area allowances are subtracted from gross income first — the slabs then apply to what's left, not your full salary.
